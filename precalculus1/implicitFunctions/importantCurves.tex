Algebraic curves have been studied for centuries. There are several curves and family of curves that we encounter in Calculus and beyond.



Conics:

\begin{example} The Circle


$(x-x_0)^2 + (y-t_0)^2 = r^2$

$(x_0, y_0)$ is the center of the circle.  $r$ is the radius

\begin{example} The Ellipse


$\left(\frac{x-x_0}{a^2}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{y-y_0}{b^2}\right)^2 = 1$

$(x_0, y_0)$ is the center of the circle.  $a$ and $b$ are the major and minor axis, depending on which is longer.




Graph of $\left(\frac{x-2}{3^2}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{y-2}{5^2}\right)^2 = 1$

\begin{example} The Parabola


$y-y_0 = a (x-x_0)^2$  or $x-x_0 = a (y-y_0)^2$






Graph of $x-2 = 3 (y-4)^2$

            every axis y label/.style={at=(current axis.above origin),anchor=south},

\end{example}
